## Access and Authorizations

<Question q="How do I log in to KSeF?">
  Log in to KSeF through the Taxpayer Application (ap.ksef.mf.gov.pl) using one of these methods:
  Trusted Profile (Profil Zaufany), e-ID, qualified signature, or KSeF certificate. Trusted Profile
  is the most convenient for most business owners — it can be set up for free via online banking or
  at a government office. API access (for software integrations) requires a certificate or token.
</Question>

<Question q="What is ZAW-FA?">
  ZAW-FA is an authorization form for using KSeF on behalf of a taxpayer. It is submitted at the tax
  office or electronically through e-Urząd Skarbowy and allows you to authorize another person —
  such as an accountant or accounting firm — to issue and receive invoices in KSeF. The
  authorization can be scoped: read-only, issue-only, or full access.
</Question>

<Question q="How do I authorize my accountant for KSeF?">
  You can authorize your accountant by submitting the ZAW-FA form at the tax office or directly in
  the Taxpayer Application KSeF through the permissions panel. The authorization specifies whether
  the accountant can only read invoices or also issue them. Your accounting office will usually ask
  you for the appropriate authorization — check with your accountant.
</Question>

<Question q="What is a KSeF certificate?">
  A KSeF certificate is an electronic identity credential used for authentication in the KSeF API.
  It is downloaded from the MF system and allows software and integrators to log in to KSeF without
  a Trusted Profile. There are two types: for the taxpayer directly (type 1) and for an authorized
  entity (type 2).
</Question>

<Question q="What is the difference between a type 1 and type 2 certificate?">
  A type 1 certificate is issued directly to the taxpayer (NIP holder) and carries the broadest
  permissions in KSeF. A type 2 certificate is intended for entities acting on behalf of the
  taxpayer — such as accounting firms or ERP systems managing multiple clients. Type 2 always
  operates within the scope of a specific authorization that must exist in KSeF.
</Question>

<Question q="What is an authentication token?">
  An authentication token is a string generated in the Taxpayer Application KSeF, used in place of a
  certificate for API authentication. Tokens are more convenient for integrators and smaller systems
  — they do not require managing PKI certificates. A token has a defined validity period and
  permission scope, and can be revoked at any time from the Taxpayer Application.
</Question>
