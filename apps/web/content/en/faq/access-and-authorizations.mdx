## Access and Authorizations

### How do I log in to KSeF?

Log in to KSeF through the Taxpayer Application (ap.ksef.mf.gov.pl) using one of these methods: Trusted Profile (Profil Zaufany), e-ID, qualified signature, or KSeF certificate. Trusted Profile is the most convenient for most business owners — it can be set up for free via online banking or at a government office.

### How do I authorize my accountant for KSeF?

You can authorize your accountant by submitting the ZAW-FA form at the tax office or directly in the Taxpayer Application KSeF through the permissions panel. The authorization specifies whether the accountant can only read invoices or also issue them. Your accounting office will usually ask you for the appropriate authorization.

### What is a KSeF certificate?

A KSeF certificate is an electronic identity credential used for authentication in the KSeF API. It is downloaded from the MF system and allows software and integrators to log in to KSeF without a Trusted Profile. There are two types: for the taxpayer directly (type 1) and for an authorized entity (type 2).
