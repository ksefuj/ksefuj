Many freelancers and sole traders (JDG — _jednoosobowa działalność gospodarcza_, Poland's sole proprietorship form) breathe a sigh of relief: "My turnover is small — the 10,000 PLN threshold doesn't apply to me." The problem is that sentence is only true if you understand what the threshold actually measures — and it's counted differently to what most people expect.

The good news: it's straightforward once explained. The catch: cross the threshold even once, and there's no going back.

---

## What the threshold is

Poland's VAT Act (Art. 145m ust. 1) introduces a **temporary exemption from the obligation to issue invoices via KSeF** (Krajowy System e-Faktur — Poland's National e-Invoicing System) for taxpayers whose combined value of sales documented by KSeF-liable invoices in a given calendar month does not exceed 10,000 PLN gross.

That's a mouthful — but it reduces to three straightforward rules covered below.

The operative word is **temporary**. The exemption exists to give micro-entrepreneurs time to implement KSeF. It is not a permanent carve-out for small businesses — it's a transitional buffer until the end of 2026.

## How to count the 10,000 PLN threshold — step by step

Three rules that govern everything:

**1. It's per calendar month.** Not per year, not per quarter, not rolling 12 months. Each month is an independent counter — starts at zero on the first, resets at month end.

**2. Amounts are gross (VAT-inclusive).** An invoice for 8,000 PLN net + 23% VAT is an invoice for 9,840 PLN gross. You count 9,840 PLN towards the threshold.

**3. Only B2B invoices subject to KSeF are counted.** Not your total revenue — only invoices issued to businesses that fall within KSeF's scope. What doesn't count is covered in the next section.

### Example — Ania, freelance graphic designer

Ania runs a sole trader business (JDG) and invoices agencies and studios. In March 2026, her billing looks like this:

| Invoice | Client       | Gross amount | Running total |
| :------ | :----------- | :----------- | :------------ |
| No. 1   | Studio AABB  | 3,200 PLN    | 3,200 PLN     |
| No. 2   | Agencja XYZ  | 2,800 PLN    | 6,000 PLN     |
| No. 3   | Sklep Online | 2,500 PLN    | 8,500 PLN     |

Total: **8,500 PLN gross** — below the threshold. Ania can continue issuing invoices outside KSeF.

Mid-month she picks up a new project and issues another invoice:

| Invoice | Client        | Gross amount  | Running total  |
| :------ | :------------ | :------------ | :------------- |
| No. 4   | Drukarnia ABC | **2,000 PLN** | **10,500 PLN** |

The running total exceeds 10,000 PLN. **Invoice No. 4 itself must go through KSeF** — not the "next one", not "from next month". This specific invoice.

<Warning>
  There is no grace period after crossing the threshold. The invoice that pushes the running total
  past 10,000 PLN must be issued via KSeF (Art. 145m ust. 2 of the VAT Act). An invoice issued
  outside the system at that point is non-compliant.
</Warning>

### Which date counts?

The threshold applies to the month in which the invoice is **issued** — not the service delivery date or the date of sale. You may issue an invoice up to the 15th of the following month (covering the previous month), but what matters is the date on the document, not when the work was actually done.

## What does NOT count towards the threshold

If you have mixed revenue streams, this section matters — you can have high total turnover but a relatively low volume of B2B invoices that fall within the threshold calculation.

The following do **not** count:

- **B2C invoices (issued to consumers)** — invoices to individuals who are not conducting business are excluded from KSeF obligations and don't count towards the threshold.
- **Fiscal till sales (kasa fiskalna)** — turnover registered through a fiscal cash register is outside KSeF's scope and excluded from the count entirely.
- **Fiscal receipts with NIP up to 450 PLN gross** — so-called simplified invoices issued as fiscal receipts with a buyer's NIP (tax identification number) up to 450 PLN. This is a distinct document category, excluded from KSeF until 31 December 2026.
- Other document types excluded from KSeF obligations under specific provisions.

<Info>
  If you run a mixed operation — serving private customers through a fiscal till while also
  invoicing businesses — only the B2B invoices count towards the 10,000 PLN threshold. Till receipts
  are irrelevant to the calculation. Your total monthly turnover may exceed 10,000 PLN while your
  KSeF-liable invoice volume stays below — and vice versa.
</Info>

## What happens when you cross the threshold

Two things to keep in mind:

**First:** from the invoice that pushes you past 10,000 PLN gross in a month, KSeF is mandatory immediately — no transition period, no additional notification required. If invoice No. 4 crosses the line, invoice No. 4 goes through KSeF.

**Second:** you cannot revert to PDFs or paper invoices, even if your sales drop back below 10,000 PLN in subsequent months. Crossing the threshold once ends the exemption — permanently, until the entire transitional period expires at the end of 2026.

In practice: cross the threshold in March 2026, and from April, May, and every month thereafter you are in KSeF — even if you issue a single 800 PLN invoice.

<Warning>
  KSeF is not an option you can toggle on and off each month. Once you've crossed the threshold,
  you're in the system.
</Warning>

## When the exemption expires

The exemption runs **until 31 December 2026** inclusive.

From **1 January 2027**, KSeF is mandatory for **all active VAT taxpayers** — regardless of monthly turnover or invoice count. If you're currently below the threshold and managing it carefully month to month, you still need to implement KSeF by the start of 2027 at the latest.

The exemption gives you time to prepare — not a way to avoid KSeF altogether.

## Common misconceptions

### "My earnings are low, so I'm definitely under the threshold"

Not necessarily. The threshold is based on the total value of **B2B invoices subject to KSeF** — not your total income. If you have several business clients and the invoices you issue them combined exceed 10,000 PLN gross in a month, you're over the threshold — even if you also have private clients who pay in cash and don't enter the calculation at all.

### "I'm on ryczałt — it doesn't apply to me"

Ryczałt ewidencjonowany is a form of PIT (personal income tax) settlement. The 10,000 PLN threshold is a VAT matter. If you are an **active VAT taxpayer** (filing VAT-7 or VAT-7K returns), your PIT settlement method changes nothing. The same threshold and rules apply to you just as they do to any other active VAT taxpayer.

### "I don't issue invoices — no problem"

If you genuinely issue no B2B invoices subject to KSeF, there's nothing to count. But if you issue even a single invoice to a business in a given month, it enters the counter.

### "After a slow month I can go back to PDFs"

No. Crossing the threshold once ends the exemption — even if the following month you invoice a total of 1,000 PLN. There is no mechanism that permits a return to paper or PDF invoices once you have exceeded 10,000 PLN gross in any single month.

### "The exemption is permanent"

The exemption expires **31 December 2026**. From 1 January 2027, all active VAT taxpayers must issue B2B invoices through KSeF — with no exceptions and no monetary threshold. This is not a permanent arrangement; it is a temporary buffer.

## Tracking the monthly threshold in practice

You don't need to manage this in your head or in a spreadsheet. Any decent invoicing application should show the total value of invoices issued in the current month. Check whether yours:

- displays invoice totals for the current calendar month,
- lets you filter B2B invoices (issued to businesses) separately from B2C and till sales.

If you work with an accountant, ask them how to monitor the threshold for your specific situation — particularly if you have mixed sales: business invoices, private customers, and a fiscal till all running simultaneously.

<Tip>
  The counter resets on the first of every month. If you're approaching 10,000 PLN gross on B2B
  invoices, start preparing for KSeF before you cross the line — not mid-invoice-session. Better to
  have the integration ready in advance than to discover the threshold mid-month.
</Tip>

## How to prepare — even if you're currently under the threshold

The exemption buys you time, not a reprieve. From 1 January 2027, KSeF covers everyone — and a December scramble is the worst-case scenario.

**What to do now:**

**Step 1: Check your invoicing software.** Contact your provider and ask directly whether it supports KSeF. Most major platforms — Fakturownia, inFakt, wFirma, Comarch, Symfonia — already do or are finalising support.

**Step 2: Validate your XML output.** Once your software generates a KSeF-format invoice, drop the file into **[ksefuj.to](/)** before submitting. The validator checks compliance against the official MF (Ministry of Finance) schema. Your file never leaves the browser — no registration, no cost.

**Step 3: Test against MF's test environment.** The Ministry of Finance provides a free sandbox at [ap-test.ksef.mf.gov.pl/web](https://ap-test.ksef.mf.gov.pl/web/). Submit invoices with dummy data there, with no consequences. Better to learn the system on test data than on live invoices.

**Step 4: Don't wait until 2027.** You have time — use it. Every month spent getting familiar with KSeF is one less month of pressure at year-end.

More practical guidance for sole traders is in [KSeF for sole traders (JDG) — the essentials](/en/blog/ksef-for-sole-traders). Common questions answered in the [FAQ](/faq).

## Summary

The 10,000 PLN threshold is a temporary buffer, not a permanent exemption. Key rules at a glance:

| Rule                                          | What it means in practice                                   |
| :-------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Monthly, not annual**                       | Each month starts from zero                                 |
| **Gross amounts**                             | VAT-inclusive — not net values                              |
| **Only KSeF-liable B2B invoices**             | Till receipts, B2C invoices, and consumer sales don't count |
| **Crossing the threshold = end of exemption** | Immediately, from that invoice, with no return              |
| **Exemption expires 31.12.2026**              | From 1 January 2027, KSeF is mandatory for everyone         |
