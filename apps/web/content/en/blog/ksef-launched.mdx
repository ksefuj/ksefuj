As of today, every B2B invoice in Poland should go through KSeF (Krajowy System e-Faktur — Poland's National e-Invoicing System). The internet is awash with "invoice apocalypse" headlines, forums are overflowing with questions, and your inbox probably is too. Before you spiral — here are three concrete things that actually matter today.

The obligation is real from 1 April 2026. That's a fact. But the regulations leave more room than the headlines suggest. Below — specifics, no drama.

For a comprehensive overview, see our [full article on KSeF from 1 April](/blog/ksef-od-1-kwietnia-2026). This post is the short version: what you need to know today — and what can safely wait until tomorrow.

---

## Penalties? Not this year

The most important piece of news today: **penalties for breaching the KSeF obligation have been deferred until 1 January 2027.** The legal basis is Art. 106gc of the Polish VAT Act.

What does this mean in practice? From today you must issue B2B invoices through KSeF (unless you qualify for an exemption — more on that in a moment). But if something goes wrong — the system rejects your invoice, you get the format wrong, your software fails — you won't face a financial penalty. Not in 2026.

<Info>
  The deferral of penalties to 2027 means that 2026 is effectively a transition period. The
  obligation applies, but enforcement of financial sanctions starts on 1 January 2027.
</Info>

This doesn't mean you can ignore KSeF. An invoice issued outside the system in breach of the obligation is formally defective — even without a penalty. Your counterparty may have issues deducting VAT from such an invoice — consult a tax adviser for specifics. And an invoice rejected by KSeF does not receive a KSeF number and is not treated as a structured invoice — regardless of sanctions.

But you have time to learn. 2026 is there so you can make mistakes without financial consequences — and learn from them.

For details on sanctions — rates, conditions, mechanics — see our [article on KSeF from 1 April](/blog/ksef-od-1-kwietnia-2026).

## Check whether you even need to — today

If the total value of your B2B invoices doesn't exceed 10,000 PLN gross per month, you can continue issuing invoices outside KSeF until the end of 2026. This is a temporary exemption under Art. 145m of the Polish VAT Act — not a permanent exclusion, but concrete breathing room for micro-businesses.

How exactly the threshold is counted, what's included and what isn't, and what happens when you cross it — [read the dedicated article](/blog/ksef-limit-10000-zl).

## Today's trap: a March invoice issued in April

Today is 1 April. You might be issuing an invoice for a service delivered in March — after all, you have until the 15th of the following month. Here's the catch: **what matters is the invoice issue date, not the period the invoice covers.**

If you issue an invoice today — even for March — the KSeF obligation already applies to you (unless you're under the 10,000 PLN threshold). It doesn't matter that the service was performed before 1 April.

Conversely: if you issued March invoices yesterday — you don't need to recall them or reissue through KSeF. They were issued before the obligation took effect and remain valid.

<Warning>
  Issue date = obligation date. If your software generates an invoice dated today — it must go
  through KSeF. No exceptions (apart from the 10,000 PLN threshold).
</Warning>

This is the most common first-day trap. If you use invoicing software, check what date it assigns to invoices generated today — and whether KSeF integration is already enabled.

## What to do now — 3 steps

**Step 1: Log in to KSeF.** Check that you have access. Go to [ksef.podatki.gov.pl](https://ksef.podatki.gov.pl/) and log in with Profil Zaufany (Poland's trusted digital identity), via mObywatel, or with a qualified electronic signature. If your invoicing software needs a token or certificate — generate one in the Aplikacja Podatnika (Taxpayer Application). Not sure which method to choose? Read our [guide to certificates and tokens](/blog/certyfikaty-vs-tokeny-ksef).

**Step 2: Validate your invoice before sending.** KSeF has no built-in validator — you'll only find out about errors when the system rejects your invoice. And a rejected invoice does not receive a KSeF number and is not treated as a structured invoice. So before you send anything, drop the XML file into [ksefuj.to](/) — the validator checks compliance with official MF requirements. No sign-up, no charge. Your file never leaves your browser. We've covered the most common errors and how to fix them in a [separate article](/blog/najczestsze-bledy-walidacji-fa3).

**Step 3: Don't panic if it doesn't work first time.** 2026 is a transition period — penalties only start from 2027. You're allowed to learn. If you have questions, check our [FAQ](/faq). And if you're integrating KSeF with your own system, our [FA(2) vs FA(3) comparison](/blog/fa2-vs-fa3-ksef) may come in handy.

---

## Summary

KSeF is live, but it's not the end of the world. Penalties deferred to 2027 give you time to learn, the 10,000 PLN threshold gives micro-businesses breathing room, and the validator at [ksefuj.to](/) catches errors before they reach the system. Don't postpone your preparations — but don't act in panic either. Steady and methodical is the best strategy for the first month with KSeF.
