Tomorrow is 1 April. Not an April Fools' joke — it's the first day of mandatory KSeF (Krajowy System e-Faktur — Poland's National e-Invoicing System) for all active VAT taxpayers. Don't panic. Here are 10 things to tick off. If you can say "yes" to each one, you're good to go.

Not sure whether KSeF applies to you at all? [Check here](/blog/ksef-od-1-kwietnia-2026). If your monthly sales don't exceed 10,000 PLN — [you may have a temporary exemption](/blog/ksef-limit-10000-zl).

---

## Checklist — 10 things to verify

### 1. Does my invoicing software support KSeF and FA(3)?

Since 1 February 2026, the only accepted format for structured invoices is FA(3). The earlier FA(2) schema is no longer accepted by the system. Contact your software provider and ask directly: "Do you support KSeF 2.0 and FA(3)?"

If the answer is "no" or "not yet" — it's time to switch. Major platforms — Fakturownia, inFakt, wFirma, Comarch — already support KSeF or are in the final stages of rollout. We've covered the differences between FA(2) and FA(3) in a [separate article](/blog/fa2-vs-fa3-ksef).

### 2. Can I log in to KSeF?

To authenticate in KSeF you need one of three things: **Profil Zaufany** (Poland's trusted identity profile — free, set up via internet banking or the mObywatel app), a qualified electronic signature, or an authorisation token.

For most sole traders (JDG — jednoosobowa działalność gospodarcza), Profil Zaufany is the simplest option. If you don't have one yet — set it up now. If you do — check that login works at [ap.ksef.mf.gov.pl](https://ap.ksef.mf.gov.pl/).

More on authentication methods in our article on [KSeF certificates and tokens](/blog/certyfikaty-vs-tokeny-ksef).

### 3. Have I generated a test invoice and validated it?

Create an invoice with fictitious data in your software. Then drop the generated XML file into **[ksefuj.to](/)** — a free validator that checks compliance with the FA(3) schema and official MF requirements. Your data never leaves your browser.

<Tip>
  KSeF 2.0 has no built-in validator. You only find out about errors when the system rejects your
  invoice — and a rejected invoice is an invoice that doesn't legally exist. Better to check
  beforehand.
</Tip>

We've covered the most common issues caught by validation in our article on [FA(3) validation errors](/blog/najczestsze-bledy-walidacji-fa3).

### 4. Have I submitted a test invoice to the MF test environment?

The Ministry of Finance provides a test environment at [ap-test.ksef.mf.gov.pl](https://ap-test.ksef.mf.gov.pl/web/). You can submit invoices with fictitious data there — with no legal consequences whatsoever. It's the best way to confirm that your software communicates correctly with KSeF.

Don't leave this test for tomorrow. Tomorrow you'll be issuing real invoices.

### 5. Do I know what to do when KSeF is unavailable?

The regulations provide two fallback modes:

- **Offline mode** — when you have no internet or KSeF is temporarily down. You issue an invoice locally and submit it to KSeF no later than **the end of the next business day**.
- **Emergency mode** — when the MF officially declares an extended system outage. You issue invoices outside KSeF and submit them after the outage ends, within **7 business days**.

Your invoicing software should handle both modes automatically — check with your provider before a crisis hits. Details in our [FAQ](/faq).

### 6. Does my accountant have access to KSeF?

If you use an accounting firm, your accountant needs formal access to KSeF on your behalf. This is done via the **ZAW-FA** form — filed at the tax office or electronically through e-Urząd Skarbowy (e-Tax Office).

<Warning>
  Without ZAW-FA (or authorisation granted in the Aplikacja Podatnika panel), your accountant has
  zero access to your invoices in KSeF — even if they've been handling your books for years. Check
  this today.
</Warning>

Your accounting firm will usually walk you through the process — but make sure it's actually done. More on permissions in the article on [KSeF certificates and tokens](/blog/certyfikaty-vs-tokeny-ksef).

### 7. Do I know what the QR code on the invoice looks like?

Invoices issued through KSeF include a QR code that enables document verification. Your invoicing software generates it automatically — you don't need to create it yourself.

Check how the print version of your invoice looks in your software and whether the QR code appears. If in doubt — ask your software provider.

### 8. Do I know where to get help?

The Ministry of Finance runs a helpdesk for KSeF-related queries. Current contact details are available at [ksef.podatki.gov.pl](https://ksef.podatki.gov.pl).

<Info>
  Save that address now — don't go looking for it in a crisis. It's also worth having the Krajowa
  Informacja Skarbowa (National Tax Information) helpline number to hand for tax questions.
</Info>

### 9. Have I notified my clients about the change?

From tomorrow, your invoices go into KSeF instead of landing in an inbox. Your clients should know — and they'll need their own KSeF access to retrieve purchase invoices.

A brief email or message now will save questions and confusion later. You can still send a PDF for your counterparty's convenience — but the legally binding document is the XML file registered in KSeF.

### 10. Do I have a plan B?

What if your software doesn't work on day one?

**Option 1:** [Aplikacja Podatnika KSeF](https://ap.ksef.mf.gov.pl/) — the free MF tool that runs in a browser. Log in with Profil Zaufany and issue an invoice manually via the form. For 2–3 invoices a month, it's more than enough.

**Option 2:** Offline mode — issue the invoice locally and submit it to KSeF by the end of the next business day. This is a lawful mechanism, not a workaround.

<Info>
  Financial penalties for breaching the KSeF obligation are deferred until 1 January 2027. But a
  rejected invoice is an invoice that doesn't legally exist — regardless of penalties. Don't wait
  for sanctions to motivate your preparation.
</Info>

## Ready?

If you answered "yes" to every point — tomorrow will be a normal working day. One invoice, one click, a KSeF number in the system.

If any answer was "no" — you still have a few hours. Start with point 3: drop your file into **[ksefuj.to](/)** and check whether your invoice is valid. No sign-up, no charge. Your data never leaves your browser.
