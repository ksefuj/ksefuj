Deep breath — it's not as daunting as it sounds.

KSeF (Krajowy System e-Faktur — Poland's National e-Invoicing System) is the government platform through which you'll be sending invoices to your clients. Instead of emailing a PDF, your invoice goes into the Ministry of Finance system, gets a unique number, and is immediately accessible to your client.

If you issue 2–3 invoices a month — your invoicing software will handle all of this for you. Your day-to-day work doesn't change. What changes is where the finished document ends up.

---

## Does KSeF apply to me?

It depends on how you handle VAT.

**You're an active VAT taxpayer** (you file VAT-7 or VAT-7K returns)? → KSeF is mandatory for you from **1 April 2026**. No exceptions, no staying with PDFs for B2B transactions.

**You're VAT-exempt** (annual turnover below 240,000 PLN, not registered as an active VAT taxpayer)? → No obligation for now. The KSeF obligation for VAT-exempt taxpayers comes into force on **1 January 2027** (for those whose monthly invoice value does not exceed 10,000 PLN). You can start testing KSeF voluntarily now.

**Your monthly sales don't exceed 10,000 PLN?** → You have a temporary exemption from KSeF — **until 31 December 2026 under current regulations**. After that date, the obligation extends to this group too. This threshold has already been amended once — always verify the current date at [ksef.podatki.gov.pl](https://ksef.podatki.gov.pl) before making any decisions.

<Info>
  The 10,000 PLN threshold is based on the total gross (including VAT) value of B2B invoices subject
  to the KSeF obligation issued in a given month. Sales through cash registers, B2C invoices (for
  private individuals), and simplified receipts with NIP up to 450 PLN do not count.
</Info>

## What do I need to do NOW — 5 steps

You still have a few days. That's plenty of time to prepare calmly.

**Step 1: Check whether your software supports KSeF**

Go to your invoicing software provider's website, or call their support line and ask directly: "Does your software support KSeF 2.0 and invoice submission from 1 April 2026?" Most popular platforms — Fakturownia, inFakt, wFirma, Comarch and others — have already added this or are finalising their implementation. If yours hasn't — now is the time to switch.

**Step 2: Issue a test invoice**

Before you send your first real invoice through KSeF, do a trial run in your software. Create an invoice with fictitious data and walk through the whole process — from creation to submission.

**Step 3: Validate your invoice at ksefuj.to**

Before sending anything to the MF system, drop the generated file into **[ksefuj.to](/)** — a free validator that checks whether your invoice meets the official MF requirements. If something's wrong, you'll know before it reaches KSeF and gets rejected.

<Tip>
  A rejected invoice is legally non-existent — even if your client already has a PDF copy by email.
  Check your file first at ksefuj.to. It takes 30 seconds and costs nothing. Your data never leaves
  your browser.
</Tip>

**Step 4: Send a test invoice to the MF test environment**

The Ministry of Finance provides a dedicated test environment at [ap-test.ksef.mf.gov.pl/web](https://ap-test.ksef.mf.gov.pl/web/). You can submit invoices with fictitious data there — no consequences whatsoever. This is a good moment to check that your software communicates correctly with the system.

**Step 5: From 1 April — issue invoices through KSeF**

From that date, every B2B invoice must go through KSeF. Your software should do this automatically — for you it's just clicking "Send".

## What if I don't have invoicing software?

You don't need to buy anything. The Ministry of Finance provides two free tools:

**Aplikacja Podatnika KSeF** — the official MF app available at [ap.ksef.mf.gov.pl](https://ap.ksef.mf.gov.pl/). It runs in a browser, no installation needed. It lets you issue invoices via a form, view received invoices, and manage permissions. You log in using Profil Zaufany (Poland's trusted identity profile — free, set up via your internet banking, the mObywatel app, or at a government office). It's the same login you'd use to view an e-prescription or file your PIT return online.

**e-mikrofirma** — a free MF application for micro-businesses, covering invoice issuance and basic tax filings. It supports KSeF in a limited capacity — check the current feature list on the MF website before choosing this tool.

If you issue a handful of invoices a month and want something simple, Aplikacja Podatnika KSeF is more than enough.

For those already using commercial software (Fakturownia, inFakt, wFirma and others) — check with your provider whether the KSeF update is included in your existing subscription. It usually is.

## What if something goes wrong?

Don't panic — the regulations include a proper escape hatch.

**No internet or KSeF temporarily unavailable?** You can issue an invoice locally and submit it to KSeF no later than **the end of the next business day**. The invoice is valid from the moment of issue — you receive the KSeF number after submission.

**Extended system outage?** If KSeF goes down for longer, the Ministry of Finance officially declares emergency mode. In this mode you can issue invoices outside the system, then submit them to KSeF within the prescribed window after the outage ends — as specified in the MF announcement. Send any outstanding invoices as soon as possible.

Serious KSeF outages are rare. The MF announces them in advance at [ksef.podatki.gov.pl](https://ksef.podatki.gov.pl) and through official channels.

<Info>
  Your invoicing software should handle both emergency modes automatically — ask your provider about
  this before a crisis arises.
</Info>

## Will my client see my invoices?

Yes — and that's good for both sides.

When your invoice is accepted by KSeF, the system issues a **UPO (Urzędowe Poświadczenie Odbioru — official receipt acknowledgement)** — essentially a delivery confirmation. The UPO contains the unique **KSeF number** assigned to your invoice. It's your proof that the invoice exists in the system.

Your client (provided they run a business and have a NIP) can download it directly from KSeF through their own account. You don't need to send them anything — the invoice is in the system and both of you have access to it.

On privacy: an invoice in KSeF goes into the Ministry of Finance government database. The tax authorities have access to it. This works exactly the same as your VAT returns — they were always accessible to the tax office. Nothing new from a tax law perspective.

<Warning>
  Keep your UPO and archive it alongside your copy of the invoice. In the event of a dispute with a
  client or a tax audit, the UPO is your proof that the invoice was correctly registered in the
  system.
</Warning>

## Common sole-trader questions about KSeF

### Do I need to buy new software?

No — if your current software has already been updated for KSeF 2.0. Check with your provider. If you don't have any software at all, the MF's Aplikacja Podatnika KSeF is free and perfectly adequate for 2–3 invoices a month.

### How do I log in to KSeF?

You log in through the [Aplikacja Podatnika KSeF](https://ap.ksef.mf.gov.pl/) using **Profil Zaufany** (Poland's trusted identity profile — free, set up via your internet banking, the mObywatel app, or at a government office). You can also use an e-ID card or a qualified electronic signature. For most sole traders, Profil Zaufany is the simplest option — if you don't have one yet, set it up now.

### What about invoices for consumers (B2C)?

Invoices issued to natural persons not conducting business (consumers) are as a rule **excluded from KSeF** — the detail is governed by Art. 106nd ust. 3 of the VAT Act and the MF Regulation of 7.12.2025. You can still issue these as PDFs or on paper — nothing changes here. KSeF primarily concerns B2B invoices, i.e. those issued to businesses and traders. If you issue invoices to consumers, consult a tax adviser about your specific situation.

### What about invoices in foreign currencies?

Invoices in euros or other currencies also go through KSeF — the system handles foreign currencies. Your invoicing software should deal with this automatically. The rules for converting amounts to PLN for VAT purposes remain unchanged. If you have doubts about a specific case, consult a tax adviser.

### When do penalties kick in?

The obligation to issue invoices through KSeF starts on **1 April 2026**. However, financial penalties for non-compliance are deferred until **1 January 2027**.

That doesn't mean you can wait. An invoice issued outside KSeF after 1 April — in breach of the obligation — may be treated as issued in violation of the regulations. That's a potential risk for both sides of the transaction. Penalties are the least of your problems.

<Warning>
  The penalty deferral is not a reason to put off your preparations. An invalid invoice means no
  payment and having to reissue it from scratch — even if the tax authority hasn't fined you yet.
</Warning>
