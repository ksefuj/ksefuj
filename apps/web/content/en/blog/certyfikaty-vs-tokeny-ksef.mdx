Before you issue your first invoice through KSeF (Krajowy System e-Faktur — Poland's National
e-Invoicing System), you need to authenticate. Simple in principle — but the system offers several
different authentication methods, each working differently and suited to a different type of user.

Profil Zaufany (Poland's trusted identity profile), e-ID card, qualified electronic signature, KSeF
certificate, token — which do you go with? And how do you know whether your invoicing software
needs something on top of all this?

If you're a freelancer or JDG (jednoosobowa działalność gospodarcza — sole trader) issuing a handful
of invoices a month, the answer takes about a minute to understand. If you run an accounting office
or are integrating KSeF with an ERP system — you'll find the right answer here too.

---

## Six methods — at a glance

KSeF supports two access modes: **manual** (you log in through a browser and work in the Aplikacja
Podatnika) and **automatic** (your software connects to KSeF via the API without your direct
involvement for every transaction). Authentication methods divide accordingly.

| Method                         | Mode    | For whom                                              | Cost                |
| ------------------------------ | ------- | ----------------------------------------------------- | ------------------- |
| Profil Zaufany                 | browser | any business owner                                    | free                |
| e-dowód (e-ID card)            | browser | anyone with an activated chip-enabled ID              | free                |
| Qualified signature / seal\*   | browser | individuals and entities with a qualified certificate | paid (subscription) |
| KSeF certificate (type 1 or 2) | API     | ERP systems, integrators                              | free                |
| Authentication token           | API     | invoicing software, integrators                       | free                |

\*Qualified seal support for legal entities should be verified in the current MF FAQ.

The rule is simple: logging in manually via the website → Profil Zaufany, e-ID card, or qualified
signature (seal support should be verified). Your software connecting to KSeF automatically → it needs a certificate or a token.

## Profil Zaufany — enough for most

Profil Zaufany is a free government digital identity. If you've ever filed a PIT return online,
checked an e-prescription, or logged in to ZUS (Poland's social security system) — you almost
certainly already have one.

If not, setting one up takes a few minutes. You can do it via:

- **internet banking** — most Polish banks support this process without leaving home,
- **the mObywatel app** on your phone,
- **ePUAP** (pz.gov.pl),
- or in person at a tax office.

In KSeF, Profil Zaufany gives full access to the Aplikacja Podatnika at
[ap.ksef.mf.gov.pl](https://ap.ksef.mf.gov.pl/). From there you can issue invoices using a form,
browse documents received from counterparties, and manage permissions for other parties — such as
your accountant or accounting office.

There is one important limitation: Profil Zaufany is for **manual login only**. Your invoicing
software — Fakturownia, inFakt, wFirma, or any other — connects to KSeF via the API, and Profil
Zaufany doesn't work there. The software needs a token or certificate instead. You don't have to
sort this yourself, though — when you first configure the integration, the software will walk you
through that step.

<Info>
  For a sole trader issuing a few invoices a month, Profil Zaufany is entirely sufficient. If you
  use a commercial invoicing platform, the token setup is a one-off task — and typically takes just
  a few minutes.
</Info>

## e-ID card — if yours is already activated

New Polish identity cards have a built-in chip that supports electronic login. If your e-dowód is
activated for electronic signature purposes, you can use it to log in to KSeF in exactly the same
way as Profil Zaufany. Activation requires a one-time visit to a government office.

In practice, the e-ID card is the less popular choice — Profil Zaufany is more convenient because
it doesn't require a card reader. But if you already have an activated e-dowód and use it elsewhere,
it works perfectly well for KSeF too.

## Qualified signature and qualified seal — for companies

A **qualified electronic signature** is the commercial equivalent of a handwritten signature —
legally valid across the European Union under the eIDAS standard. It's issued by commercial
providers including Certum, Sigillum, and EuroCert, on annual or multi-year subscriptions.
It costs money.

In the context of KSeF, a qualified signature gives the same browser-based access as Profil Zaufany
— full access to the Aplikacja Podatnika. Who should use this? Primarily anyone who already holds a
qualified signature for other purposes: e-KRS filings, public procurement, signing contracts. If you
don't have one and don't need it elsewhere — Profil Zaufany is cheaper and equally effective.

The eIDAS standard distinguishes between a qualified signature (an instrument for a natural person)
and a **qualified seal** (an instrument for a legal entity, such as a company). Whether and to what
extent KSeF supports the qualified seal as a distinct login method — always verify in the current
Ministry of Finance FAQ or the Aplikacja Podatnika KSeF 2.0 manual.

<Info>
  The current list of supported authentication methods — including any support for qualified seals
  for legal entities — should always be verified in the official [MF
  FAQ](https://ksef.podatki.gov.pl/pytania-i-odpowiedzi-ksef-20). That is the authoritative source.
</Info>

## KSeF certificate — two types, two use cases

A KSeF certificate is an electronic identity credential used for API access — for software and
systems that communicate with KSeF automatically, without a human involved in every operation.
You obtain it from the Ministry of Finance system.

There are two types that differ significantly.

**Type 1** is issued directly to the taxpayer — the NIP holder. It carries the broadest permissions
within that NIP. It's used when a company's own system integrates with KSeF under the company's own
identity — for example, when a company connects its ERP directly to KSeF as itself, not through an
intermediary.

**Type 2** is intended for entities acting on behalf of a taxpayer: accounting offices, multi-client
systems, external integrators. The key rule: a type 2 certificate always operates within the context
of a specific authorisation. Without a valid mandate from the taxpayer — such as a ZAW-FA or a
permission granted in the Aplikacja Podatnika panel — a type 2 certificate simply won't work.

In short: **type 1 — when the system operates under the company's own NIP, type 2 — when it operates
on behalf of someone else's NIP.**

<Warning>
  The details of the certificate download process may change with Ministry of Finance system
  updates. Before you begin, check the current instructions in the Aplikacja Podatnika KSeF 2.0
  manual, available at ksef.podatki.gov.pl.
</Warning>

## Authentication token — the simplest API access

An authentication token is a string — an access key that you generate yourself inside the Aplikacja
Podatnika KSeF. It requires no certificate infrastructure to manage. Generate it in the app, enter
it in your software's configuration, and you're done.

A token has a defined validity period and permission scope — you can restrict it to invoice issuance
only, or to read-only access. You can revoke it at any time from the Aplikacja Podatnika with a
single click. That matters: if you change your software provider or something goes wrong, revoking
access is instant and straightforward.

When should you choose a token over a certificate? Tokens are more convenient for integrators and
smaller systems, because they don't require PKI infrastructure setup. For complex deployments serving
multiple taxpayers simultaneously, a certificate may be the better solution — more durable
credentials and a broader control surface.

<Tip>
  If your software asks you to "configure a KSeF connection" or "enter a token" — log in to the
  [Aplikacja Podatnika](https://ap.ksef.mf.gov.pl/), find the token management section and generate
  a new one. The software will usually walk you through this step by step.
</Tip>

## ZAW-FA — when someone acts on your behalf

If you use an accounting office or want to give someone else access to your KSeF — you need a
ZAW-FA authorisation.

ZAW-FA is a formal authorisation form. It allows you to authorise a specific person or entity — your
bookkeeper or accounting office, for example — to issue and receive invoices on your behalf. You can
submit the authorisation in several ways:

- **in person at a tax office**,
- **via e-Urząd Skarbowy** ([podatki.gov.pl](https://www.podatki.gov.pl/e-urzad-skarbowy/)) — electronically,
- **directly in the Aplikacja Podatnika KSeF** — via the permissions panel, without leaving the system.

You can restrict the scope of access to three variants: read-only, issue-only, or full access. It's
worth agreeing the scope with your accountant before granting permissions — better to match access to
actual needs than to hand over broader rights than necessary.

Good news for sole traders: if you work with an accounting office, they will typically ask you for
the appropriate authorisation and guide you through the whole process. You don't need to know the
details — just ask your accountant.

For accounting offices and their clients, this is the key rule: a ZAW-FA from every client is a
prerequisite before any API integration can be activated. A type 2 certificate without an active
authorisation simply won't work.

## Which option is right for you?

The table below is practical guidance — not legal advice. Always verify the current position in the
[MF FAQ](https://ksef.podatki.gov.pl/pytania-i-odpowiedzi-ksef-20).

| Your situation                                                | Recommended method                                                              |
| ------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Sole trader, issuing invoices manually in Aplikacja Podatnika | **Profil Zaufany**                                                              |
| Using invoicing software (Fakturownia, inFakt, wFirma etc.)   | **Profil Zaufany** for manual login + **token** generated for the software      |
| Company, own ERP integrating with KSeF                        | **KSeF certificate type 1** or token                                            |
| Accounting office serving multiple clients                    | **ZAW-FA** from each client + **type 2 certificate** (or token)                 |
| Developer integrating KSeF into a company system              | **Token** (easy start) or **type 1/2 certificate** (production)                 |
| Already have a qualified signature for other purposes         | You can use it instead of Profil Zaufany — gives identical browser-based access |

## e-mikrofirma — if simplicity is the priority

If you issue invoices occasionally and would rather focus on your work than on configuring access —
take a look at **e-mikrofirma**. It's a free, browser-based Ministry of Finance application for the
smallest businesses, covering basic invoicing and tax filings.

e-mikrofirma supports KSeF in a limited capacity. Before choosing this tool, check the current
feature list on the MF website — the scope of KSeF support may change with application updates. For
those issuing invoices occasionally, it may well be sufficient and the simplest available option.

More about preparing for KSeF as a sole trader in the article
[KSeF from 1 April 2026 — what changes](/blog/ksef-od-1-kwietnia-2026).

## Before your invoice hits the system — validate it first

Picking your authentication method is only half the job. Before your invoice reaches KSeF — make
sure it's correct. An invoice rejected by the system is an invoice that legally doesn't exist —
even if your client already received it by email.

Drop the XML file into **[ksefuj.to](/)** — a free validator built to the official MF specification.
It runs entirely in your browser: your file never leaves your browser. You get a result in seconds.
