KSeF (Krajowy System e-Faktur — Poland's National e-Invoicing System) is live — but before you send your first real invoice, run through the whole process in the sandbox. The Ministry of Finance provides a test environment where you can submit invoices with dummy data, with no legal consequences whatsoever. Fifteen minutes from now, you'll know exactly what to expect.

This guide takes you from login, through submission, to confirming receipt on the buyer's side.

---

## Before you start

Make sure you have:

- **Invoicing software with KSeF 2.0 integration** — or the free [Aplikacja Podatnika](https://ap.ksef.mf.gov.pl/) from the Ministry of Finance (browser-based, no installation needed)
- **Profil Zaufany, e-Dowód, or a qualified certificate** — to authenticate in KSeF (Profil Zaufany is the simplest option — if you don't have one, [set it up via online banking](https://www.gov.pl/web/gov/zaloz-profil-zaufany))
- **Your company's NIP** (Polish tax identification number) — you log in with your real NIP, even in the test environment
- **15 minutes**

<Info>
  The test environment is a sandbox — nothing you do there has any legal standing. You can submit
  invoices with made-up amounts and buyer details. None of it reaches the tax office.
</Info>

---

## Step 1: Log in to the KSeF test environment

Go to [ap-test.ksef.mf.gov.pl](https://ap-test.ksef.mf.gov.pl/web/) — the official KSeF 2.0 test environment.

1. Click "Zaloguj się" (Log in).
2. Choose your authentication method — Profil Zaufany, e-Dowód, or qualified certificate.
3. Enter your company's NIP.
4. Once logged in, you'll see a panel with two tabs: **outbox** (invoices you've sent) and **inbox** (invoices sent to you).

If this is your first login, both tabs will be empty. That's expected.

<Warning>
  Make sure you're logging in to the **test** environment (ap-test.ksef.mf.gov.pl), not the
  production one. Invoices submitted to production have real legal consequences.
</Warning>

---

## Step 2: Issue a test invoice

Open your invoicing software (or the Aplikacja Podatnika) and create a new invoice. It doesn't need to be real — this is a test.

1. **Enter dummy details** — any service name, amount, and date will do.
2. **Use a fellow business owner's NIP as the buyer's NIP** — in step 5 you'll check whether the invoice arrived in their account. If you're testing solo, any valid NIP works (e.g. the Ministry of Finance NIP: `5260250274`).
3. **Add one line item** — "Test service", 100 PLN net, 23% VAT rate is plenty.
4. **Confirm the software is pointed at the test environment** — most tools let you switch between production and test in the KSeF integration settings.
5. **Click "Send to KSeF"** (or the equivalent in your software).

The software generates the XML file and submits it to the test environment. You don't need to touch the file — the software handles that.

<Tip>
  In the MF Aplikacja Podatnika, you create the invoice directly in the form — you'll find it after
  logging in, under "Wystaw fakturę" (Issue invoice). No external software needed.
</Tip>

---

## Step 3: Collect the UPO

After submission, KSeF returns a **UPO (Urzędowe Poświadczenie Odbioru — Official Proof of Receipt)**. This is the system's formal acknowledgement that your invoice was accepted.

The UPO contains:

- **KSeF number** — your invoice's unique identifier in the system
- **Date and time of acceptance** — from this moment, the invoice formally exists in KSeF
- **File hash and system digital signature** — proof the document hasn't been altered in transit

Find the UPO in your software — it typically appears alongside the invoice as a "Sent" or "Accepted by KSeF" status, with an option to download the details.

<Warning>
  Without a UPO, you have no confirmation that KSeF accepted the invoice. If you see an error
  message instead of a confirmation — the invoice was rejected. Check the status and jump to "What
  to do if submission fails" below.
</Warning>

## Step 4: Check the KSeF number

Every invoice accepted by KSeF receives a **35-character KSeF number** — a long string of digits and letters, completely separate from your own invoice number (e.g. FV/001/04/2026).

Check that it's visible:

- **In your software** — it should appear alongside the invoice, next to or below the document number.
- **On the printout / PDF** — if you generate a visual invoice, the KSeF number should appear on it, labelled "Numer KSeF" or "Numer identyfikujący fakturę w KSeF".

<Info>
  The KSeF number is the government system's identifier. Your invoice number (e.g. FV/001/04/2026)
  is your internal reference. Both are required, but they serve different purposes — don't confuse
  them.
</Info>

---

## Step 5: Verify receipt on the buyer's side

This is the most illuminating part of the test — you'll see what the invoice looks like from your client's perspective.

If you used a fellow business owner's NIP in step 2:

1. Ask them to log in to the test environment at [ap-test.ksef.mf.gov.pl](https://ap-test.ksef.mf.gov.pl/web/) — using their own NIP.
2. Have them open the **inbox**.
3. Your test invoice should be there — with the KSeF number, amounts, and your NIP as the issuer.

<Info>
  The buyer doesn't need to receive anything by email or any other channel. KSeF delivers the
  invoice directly to the account linked to their NIP. Once they log in to KSeF — it's already
  there.
</Info>

This is the new model: **the invoice goes into the system and both parties have access to it.** No more "I never received your invoice email".

<Tip>
  Testing solo and don't have a second NIP? The UPO from step 3 is sufficient proof that KSeF
  accepted the invoice. You can verify the buyer's receipt separately — for instance with an
  accountant who handles your client's books.
</Tip>

## What to do if submission fails

The three most common causes:

### 1. Authentication error or expired session

KSeF sessions expire after a set time. If your software shows an authentication error:

- Refresh the KSeF connection in your software settings.
- Log in again.
- Retry the submission.

### 2. Invalid buyer NIP

KSeF validates NIP format. If you entered a string of digits that isn't a valid NIP:

- Verify the NIP at [biznes.gov.pl](https://biznes.gov.pl).
- Correct the NIP on the invoice and resubmit.

### 3. Malformed XML file

This is rare with decent software — your tool should generate valid XML automatically. But if you see a raw error message:

- Paste the file into [ksefuj.to](/) — you'll get a clear description of every problem in plain language. Your data never leaves your browser.
- Fix the flagged issues in your software and resubmit.

## Done — what next?

You've just completed the full invoice lifecycle in KSeF: issue → submit → UPO → buyer confirmation. You know how it works. There was nothing scary about it.

When you issue your first real invoice:

1. Switch your software to the **production** KSeF environment.
2. Make sure the buyer's details — NIP, name, address — are correct.
3. Submit the invoice and check the UPO, exactly as you did in this test.

Everything else works identically.
